Earthquake Preparedness Guide

Important: Earthquakes can strike without warning.Prepare now to protect yourself, your family, and your property.

Before an Earthquake: How to Prepare

  • Secure Heavy Objects: Anchor heavy furniture, appliances, and shelves to walls to prevent tipping or falling.
  • Practice "Drop, Cover, and Hold On": Conduct regular drills so everyone knows what to do during an earthquake.
  • Identify Safe Spots: Know where to go during an earthquake, such as under sturdy furniture or against an interior wall.
  • Prepare an Emergency Kit: Include water, food, first aid supplies, flashlight, batteries, and important documents in your kit.

During an Earthquake:What to Do

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On: Drop to your hands and knees, cover your head and neck, and hold on until the shaking stops.
  • Avoid Windows: Stay away from windows, glass, and anything that could shatter or fall.
  • If Outdoors: Move to an open area away from buildings, trees, and power lines.
  • If in a Vehicle: Pull over to a safe area, away from overpasses, and remain in the vehicle until the shaking stops.

After an Earthquake: Stay Safe

  • Check for Injuries: Provide first aid if needed and call for emergency assistance if necessary.
  • Inspect for Damage: Check your home for structural damage, gas leaks, or water line breaks, and evacuate if it's unsafe.
  • Be Prepared for Aftershocks: Aftershocks may follow the main earthquake, so stay alert and prepared to "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" again.
  • Stay Informed: Listen to emergency broadcasts for updates on conditions and safety instructions.

Emergency Kit Checklist

  • Water (3-day supply for each person)
  • Non-perishable food (3-day supply)
  • First aid kit and essential medications
  • Flashlight and extra batteries
  • Important documents (IDs, insurance, etc.) in a waterproof bag
  • Blankets, extra clothing, and sturdy shoes
  • Whistle to signal for help
  • Mobile phone and portable charger
